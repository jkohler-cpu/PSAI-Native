2
Proficiency Track
Delivery · GTM · Leaders  ·  Trailhead · Slack · Yoodli  ·  Three paths, shared foundation
Delivery path

Three acts aligned to the MVG delivery arc — Before You Build, While You're Delivering, With the Customer & Beyond. Builds practiced judgment across all 11 clusters. Opens with a diagnostic; closes with a forward brief.

GTM path

Shares Act 1 (pre-engagement skills) with the delivery path, then diverges into a GTM-specific module covering scoping integrity, MVG delivery literacy, and the GTM-to-delivery handoff. Yoodli for customer scoping and readiness conversations. Closes with a shared forward brief aligned to the delivery team taking over.

Leader path (Phase 2 of the Leadership Progression)

The fluency and judgment phase of the leadership arc — building confident human-AI partnership. Trailhead for tool selection, discernment, and responsible use; Slack canvases with leader-specific decision protocols; Yoodli for coaching conversations and team AI accountability discussions. Connects forward to Phase 3: AI-Native Leadership.

Modality stack
  • Trailhead — structured learning paths for each skill cluster; concept + scenario + checkpoint
  • Slack always-on canvases — reference cards, decision protocols, and anti-pattern guides in the flow of work
  • Yoodli — communicative clusters: Customer AI Readiness conversations, Outcome Framing, scoping handoff
  • Opening diagnostic + closing forward brief
  • Peer exchange at Act 2 midpoint (delivery) / GTM module midpoint
After this tier — delivery Practitioner can write defensible intent, validate AI outputs against a rubric, and make calibrated human-AI delegation decisions on an active engagement.
After this tier — GTM Seller can scope AI-native engagements honestly against MVG readiness criteria, frame outcomes rather than deliverables, and hand off to delivery with the information they need to succeed.
After this tier — Leaders Leader has genuine fluency working alongside AI — can make calibrated tool and judgment decisions, coach team members through adoption from a place of earned credibility, and is ready to engage Phase 3 of the Leadership Progression.
3
Immersive Cohort
Quantum Leap pilot teams  ·  Cohort-based, embedded  ·  Multi-week, aligned to engagement arc
What it builds

Real-stakes application of all skills under live delivery conditions, with facilitation and coaching support. This is where learning becomes practice — pressure scenarios, mock projects, co-creation workshops, and on-project reinforcement. It also generates the Salesforce-specific field artifacts the Proficiency Track currently needs.

Format & Assets
  • Live facilitated workshops — in-person or virtual
  • Mock projects and pressure scenarios (time constraint + stakeholder pressure)
  • Customer co-creation session simulation
  • On-project reinforcement with Agentic Coach presence
  • Post-project debrief and learning capture
After this tier Practitioner has applied all skills under real conditions — navigated system interdependencies, facilitated co-creation, and maintained scrutiny under pressure.
4
Agentic Coach Track
Agentic Coaches & Player Coaches  ·  2-day in-person intensive + ongoing protocols
What it builds

Coaching and facilitation skills for sustaining AI-native delivery quality in the flow of work. Emphasis on coaching Discernment and Intent Writing — the hardest skills to coach because they require a different approach than technical coaching. The 2-day intensive includes live facilitation practice and peer coaching protocol development.

Format & Assets
  • 2-day in-person intensive — facilitation and coaching practice
  • Per-cluster coach guides and coaching prompts
  • Anti-pattern playbook with in-flight recognition signals
  • Peer Coach Community (#ql-learnings-feedback)
  • Quarterly Agentic Coach retrospective
After this tier Coach can recognize skill gaps in the flow of work, use targeted coaching prompts, facilitate discernment reflection, and hold the execution model standards without killing team momentum.

Why some clusters don't reach the Coach Track

Data Quality Judgment and Customer AI Readiness Assessment are pre-engagement diagnostic skills — they happen before the team forms, so there's no live coaching context. Creative Direction & Taste is highly individual and context-specific; coaches recognize it in the field but it doesn't lend itself to a formal coaching protocol. These three clusters max out at Applied in Tier 3.

Leadership Progression

A dedicated arc for PS people managers — from AI fluency in their own role today, through confident human-AI partnership, to leading in an AI-native organization. Three phases that span the program tiers.

Design principle: personal fluency before coaching

Leaders cannot coach AI adoption on their teams if they haven't worked through it themselves. The progression is intentionally sequenced — Phase 1 builds personal confidence through practical application before Phase 2 shifts attention to partnering with AI, and Phase 3 only becomes meaningful once a leader has genuine fluency to draw on. Leaders who skip to Phase 3 without Phases 1 and 2 lead through AI-native change in the abstract. Leaders who work through the arc lead from experience.

Build Sequence

The recommended order for building out assets — by program tier, not skill cluster number. Highest leverage first.

Phase 1
Proficiency Slack canvases
All 11 clusters
Always-on Slack reference canvases for all 11 clusters — the in-the-moment layer of the Proficiency Track. One canvas per cluster: concept, observable behaviors, decision protocol, and anti-patterns. These are what consultants reach for mid-engagement, not what they read in a course. Fast to build, immediately deployable, and they feed directly into the Trailhead and Yoodli layers above them.
Intent Writing Discernment Output Validation Human-AI Orchestration + 7 more
Phase 2
Proficiency Track
Priority 4 clusters
Practice scenarios and analytic rubrics for the four highest-urgency clusters for pilot delivery. These are the skills where a gap causes the most direct delivery failure — and where the Agentic Coach needs coaching protocols before the next cohort launches.
Intent Writing Discernment Output Validation Human-AI Orchestration
Phase 3
Proficiency Track
Remaining clusters
Extend the Proficiency Track to cover the remaining 7 clusters. These are still important — they fill out the full program and cover pre-engagement and human-factors skills — but they are not blocking the next pilot cohort launch.
Outcome Framing Systems Thinking Change Co-Creation Data Quality Judgment Customer AI Readiness Creative Direction Adaptive Learning
Phase 4
Immersive Cohort
All clusters
Live simulation builds — mock projects, pressure scenarios, co-creation workshops. These take longer to design and require live test conditions. They also depend on field artifacts from the active pilot cohort. Build in parallel with Phase 3 as pilot evidence comes in.

Coach Track
8 coachable clusters
Coaching protocols, coach guides, and the anti-pattern playbook for the 8 clusters that reach the Coach Track. Build last; design the 2-day intensive in parallel with Phase 4.
